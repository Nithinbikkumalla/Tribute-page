A. P. J ABDUL KALAM

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

  • Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam
  • Born: 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
  • Died: 27 July 2015
  • Profession: Indian aerospace scientist and politician
  • 11th President of India: Served from 2002 to 2007
  • Education: Studied physics and aerospace engineering
  • Career:
    • Spent four decades as a scientist and science administrator
    • Worked at Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)
    • Worked at Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)
    • Intimately involved in India's civilian space program
    • Contributed to military missile development efforts
  • Contributions:
    • Known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology
    • Played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998
    • Promoted the use of technology for national development
    • Advocated for improving healthcare and education through science and technology
  • Legacy:
    • Remembered as a people's president who inspired millions through his simplicity and dedication
    • Authored several books, including 'Wings of Fire' and 'India 2020'
    • Recipient of numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor
